What Is Racial Inequality?

Racial inequality in turn can be defined as the limited economic and social opportunities that are distributed along racial lines.



African-Americans comprise only 13% of the U.S. population and 14% of the monthly drug users, but are 37% of the people arrested for drug-related offenses in America.



African Americans make up 57% of the people in state prisons for drug offenses.



A survey in 2011 revealed that 52% of non-Hispanic whites expressed anti-Hispanic attitudes.

How to stand up for racial inequality



Vote for people you believe will help your country make everyone feel equal and welcome. Don't support places that are racist. Go to marches and be a racial equality activist!
