What is criminal justice?

Criminal justice is the delivery of justice to those who have committed crimes. The criminal justice system is a series of government agencies and institutions whose goals are to identify and catch unlawful individuals to inflict a form of punishment on them.

Important Information About Criminal Injustice



  • School to Prison Pipeline

    • This is a process where students are pushed out of school and into prison. In other words imprisoning students (Youth) due to some basic principles and policies in school that they broke. Once they are put into contact with law enforcement for disciplinary reasons, many are then pushed out of the educational environment and into the juvenile and criminal justice systems. The policies and practices that maintains the school-to-prison pipeline system is the Zero Tolerance Policy that approves harsh punishments for both minor and major violations of students from schools through suspensions, expulsions or even the presence of police on campus as School Resource Officers. Troublingly, this disparity begins as early as preschool. According to the U.S. Department of Education's Office for Civil Rights about 5% of white students have been suspended during their schooling experience, compared with 16% of Black students. This means Black students are more than three times as likely to be suspended than their white peers. American Indians also face inflated suspension rates. They represent 2 percent of out-of-school suspensions, which is 4 times greater than the percentage of total enrolled students that they comprise.

  • Drug War
    According to the Bureau of Prisons, there are 207,847 people incarcerated in federal prisons and nearly 80% of these people in federal prison and almost 60% of people in state prison for drug offenses are black or Latino which is 166,277. The war on drugs costs the US billions of dollars a year and forces police to focus on nonviolent drug offenders, rather than on violent criminals. Drug arrests have significantly contributed to our growing, unsustainable incarceration rates.
